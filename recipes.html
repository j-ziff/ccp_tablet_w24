Homemade Mac & Cheese

Origin: Northern Europe

Source: Delish.com

Category: Main Dish

Macaroni and cheese, commonly known as mac & cheese, is a comfort food staple that has captured the hearts of people all over the world. Originating from Europe and popularized in the United States, this dish consists of elbow macaroni pasta smothered in a creamy cheese sauce. The simplicity of its ingredients—pasta, cheese, and often a touch of butter or milk—belies a rich, complex flavor that has made it a favorite for both quick weeknight dinners and festive holiday gatherings.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) Butter, plus more for baking dish
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 lb. Elbow macaroni
  • 1/2 cup All-purpose flour
  • 5 cups Whole milk
  • 1 tsp. Mustard powder
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 12 oz. (about 3 cups) Shredded Cheddar
  • 8 oz. (about 2 cups) Shredded Gruyère
  • 3 oz. (about 1 1/2 cups) Finely grated Parmesan, divided
  • 1 cup Panko bread crumbs
  • 3 tbsp. Extra-virgin olive oil

Recipe Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375°. Grease a 13"x9" baking dish with butter. In a large pot of boiling salted water, cook macaroni, stirring occasionally, until al dente, 5 to 6 minutes. Drain.
  2. In a large saucepan over medium heat, melt 1 stick butter. Sprinkle flour over and cook, stirring, until slightly golden, 2 to 3 minutes. Pour in milk and whisk until combined. Add mustard powder; season with salt and pepper. Bring to a simmer over medium-high heat and cook, stirring, until sauce starts to thicken, about 2 minutes.
  3. Remove pan from heat and whisk in cheddar, Gruyère, and 1 cup Parmesan until melted and smooth. Stir in macaroni and transfer to the prepared dish.
  4. In a small bowl, combine panko, oil, and remaining 1/2 cup Parmesan. Sprinkle over macaroni; season with more pepper.
  5. Bake mac and cheese until bubbly and golden, 25 to 30 minutes. Let cool for 10 minutes.

Swedish Pancakes

Origin: Sweden

Source: True-North-Kitchen.com

Category: Breakfast

Swedish pancakes (Pannkaka [singular] or Pannkakor [plural] in Swedish) are one of the most well-known Scandinavian delicacies, and rightfully so. Tender, buttery and often served with butter and lingonberry jam, there is much to account for their popularity.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 6 tablespoons water
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter melted and cooled, plus more for the pan
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla

Recipe Steps

  1. Combine all ingredients in a blender and blend until completely smooth.Transfer to the refrigerator to rest for at least 2 hours or up to 2 days.
  2. Preheat the oven to 200 degrees if you plan to eat the pannkakor right away. Heat a 10 inch nonstick skillet over medium heat. Stir the batter briefly to recombine. Brush skillet with melted butter. Pick the skillet up off of the burner and quickly add 1/4 cup of batter. Immediately begin swirling the batter around the skillet so that it coats the bottom of the pan. Continue swirling until the batter is just set. Return skillet to the burner. Cook until the pancake is golden brown in spots on the bottom, about 30 seconds. Use a thin spatula to loosen the pancake, flip it over continue to cook on the other side for about 30 seconds. Transfer to a plate. Cover with foil and transfer to oven to keep warm and repeat with remaining batter, wiping out skillet in between pancakes with paper towel as needed.
  3. Serve with lingonberry jam and butter or other desired toppings and/or fillings.

Zucchini Fritters

Origin: Ottoman Empire

Source: NiftyRecipe.com

Category: Side Dish

Crispy zucchini fritters are the most delicate, tasty and light dish from this summer vegetable. Fresh greenery and garlic give zucchini pancakes a special taste and aroma. Appetizing soft fritters will taste even those who are indifferent to zucchini. Such a dish is ideal for breakfast or a light snack for half a day. It is desirable to use young zucchini with a thin peel and unripe seeds. If desired, grated hard cheese and finely cut onions can be added to the finished dough. Wheat flour in this recipe can be replaced by rice flour or use semolina in the same proportions.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 1 Zucchini
  • 3 Eggs
  • 3 tbsp Whole wheat flour
  • 30 ml Vegetable oil
  • 1 clove Garlic
  • 15 g Dill
  • 15 g Parsley
  • Salt to taste

Recipe Steps

  1. In a deep bowl, combine 3 chicken eggs, salt and black ground pepper.
  2. Whip the mixture with a wire whisk until homogeneous consistency.
  3. Wash dill and parsley, finely chop and add to the egg mixture. So that the zucchini fritters become more fragrant, add a garlic clove passed through the press.
  4. Wash the zucchini and grind it on a large grater.
  5. Squeeze the grated zucchini from excess juice, then add to the rest of the ingredients.
  6. Add sifted wheat flour to the obtained mixture.
  7. Mix the mixture thoroughly. Ready dough for fritters should have thick sour cream consistency.
  8. On a frying pan with heated vegetable oil, put a tablespoon of dough, forming small round fritters.
  9. Fry zucchini fritters, the recipe is simple and fast, on a medium heat until golden brown on both sides.
  10. Transfer the finished products on a plate covered with paper towels to remove excess oil. Serve ready-made zucchini fritters in warm with sour cream or favorite sauce.
